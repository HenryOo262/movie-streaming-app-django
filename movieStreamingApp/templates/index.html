To help you navigate your viewing choices, we provide clear explanations of movie ratings:

  • G (General Audience): Films rated G are suitable for viewers of all ages. These movies are family-friendly and contain no content that would be considered inappropriate for children. They are designed to be enjoyable for everyone, making them perfect for a family movie night.
  • PG (Parental Guidance): Movies with a PG rating may contain some material that parents might find unsuitable for younger children. While these films are generally appropriate for children, they might include mild language, brief nudity, or moderate action. Parental guidance is recommended to ensure the content aligns with your family’s values.
  • PG-13 (Parents Strongly Cautioned): This rating suggests that some material may be inappropriate for children under 13. PG-13 films may contain more intense themes, stronger language, or extended violence. Parents are strongly cautioned to provide appropriate guidance for younger viewers.
  • R (Restricted): Films rated R are restricted to viewers over the age of 17 or 18. These movies may include explicit language, strong sexual content, or intense violence. Viewer discretion is advised, and parental guidance is not sufficient for younger audiences.
  • NC-17 (No One 17 and Under Admitted): The NC-17 rating is reserved for movies with explicit content that is only suitable for adults. These films may contain graphic sexual content or other adult themes, and no one under the age of 17 is admitted. This rating ensures that viewers are fully aware of the mature nature of the content.
